Strategic Transformation

Intel Sells 51% of Altera to Silver Lake for $4.5B

Semiconductors FPGA Divestiture

Intel agrees to sell controlling 51% stake in Altera to Silver Lake for $4.5 billion, valuing the FPGA business at $8.75 billion. Transaction creates largest pure-play FPGA company while enabling Intel to focus on core business and strengthen balance sheet.

Strategic Rationale

Intel Focus Strategy: Divestiture enables Intel to concentrate resources on core CPU business and foundry services strategy. Sale provides $4.5B cash infusion to support Intel 18A process development and manufacturing expansion.

Value Realization: Original 2015 acquisition cost $16.7B; current $8.75B valuation reflects FPGA market challenges and Intel's execution difficulties. Silver Lake partnership positions Altera for independent growth and market leadership.

FPGA Market Dynamics

Market Opportunity: FPGA market projected to grow from $12.1B in 2023 to $25.8B by 2029, driven by AI acceleration, 5G infrastructure, and edge computing applications requiring programmable logic solutions.

Competitive Position: Altera becomes largest pure-play FPGA company, competing directly with AMD's Xilinx division. Independent structure enables focused product development and customer-centric innovation.

Partnership Structure

Operational Independence: Silver Lake's 51% controlling stake provides strategic guidance while maintaining Intel's 49% participation in upside. New CEO Raghib Hussain (ex-Marvell) brings specialized semiconductor leadership.

Manufacturing Partnership: Altera continues as Intel foundry customer, ensuring production continuity while benefiting from Intel's advanced process technology capabilities for next-generation FPGA development.

Technology Valuation Framework

Investment banking perspectives on technology sector-specific metrics

Enterprise SaaS

  • Revenue Multiples: 8-20x ARR (growth and retention dependent)
  • Key Metrics: Net Revenue Retention (120%+ premium), Magic Number, LTV:CAC
  • Growth Premiums: 40%+ ARR growth commands 25-35x multiples
  • Vertical Leadership: Market-leading positions justify 30-50% premium

Infrastructure Software

  • EV/Revenue: 6-15x (mission-critical applications premium)
  • Recurring Revenue: 80%+ recurring commands 40% premium
  • Customer Concentration: Diversified base reduces risk discount
  • Integration Value: Platform plays valued 20-30% above point solutions

Semiconductor Technology

  • EV/Sales: 3-8x (technology differentiation premium)
  • IP Portfolio: Patent strength drives 25-40% valuation premium
  • End Market Exposure: AI/5G/automotive applications valued highly
  • Manufacturing Model: Fabless models command design premium

Cybersecurity Platforms

  • Revenue Multiples: 10-25x (threat landscape urgency)
  • Platform Integration: Comprehensive platforms vs. point solutions
  • Customer Stickiness: High switching costs support premium multiples
  • Zero Trust Architecture: Next-gen security commands 40%+ premium
